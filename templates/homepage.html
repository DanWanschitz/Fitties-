Welcome to
Fit Brain Lab!

The Fit Brain Lab focuses on developing exercise-based strategies to enhance stress resilience, particularly in students. Our research explores how physical exercise influences brain mechanisms that help protect against stress.

Our Goal

At The Fit Brain Lab, our goal is to bridge the gap between physical exercise and mental well-being by exploring how physical activity can increase stress resilience, particularly among students. We aim to develop and implement evidence-based strategies that enhance mental health through exercise, ensuring that students can thrive in both academic and personal aspects of their lives.

Our Mission

The mission of The Fit Brain Lab is to optimize existing physical exercise strategies and develop innovative approaches that specifically target stress resilience, with a focus on students. By enhancing the biological mechanisms in the brain that are influenced by exercise, we strive to provide effective tools for students to manage stress and improve their mental well-being.

Recent Projects

StayFine
StayFine is a study to prevent relapse in adolescents (13–21) recovering from depression or anxiety. Using a personalized app, participants are tracked over three years through interviews, surveys, wearables and daily assessments. We explore the role of physical activity in staying well.
Partners: GGZ Oost Brabant, Amsterdam UMC and University of Groningen.

LEOPARD PREDICT
This study aims to predict antidepressant treatment response in depressed adults (18–60) within two weeks using brain imaging (rsfMRI). We also explore whether adding physical activity data improves prediction accuracy.
Partners: Amsterdam UMC and RadboudUMC.

STRIDE-4-Mental Health
STRIDE-4 studies how exercise helps university students manage stress, mental health and academics. We analyze factors that support lasting exercise habits and will develop an online intervention with education, planning and peer support.
Partners: USC Amsterdam, VU Sport Center, HvA and ROCs Amsterdam & Flevoland.
